Our Story

The Story of SYKO

SYKO didn't start as a restaurant.

It started as a table.

A family table, where food was more than just a meal — it was a way to connect, to celebrate, and to bring two cultures together. At that table, Syrian dishes met Korean flavors. Different traditions, different techniques — but the same feeling: warmth, generosity, and love through food.

From History to Home

In the early 1880s, Syrians began migrating to the United States, building communities like Little Syria in New York. By 1884, the first Koreans arrived, forming their own roots across Queens and Manhattan.

Two cultures, two journeys — both finding a home in New York.

Our Beginning

Years later, those same two cultures came together again — not in history books, but in one family. At home, meals became a fusion of identity. A Syrian table with Korean touches. A Korean dish with a Syrian soul.

What if we created a place that feels like this table?

2022

SYKO — A Place Like Home

SYKO opened in Brooklyn. Not just as a restaurant — but as a reflection of who we are. A place where Syrian and Korean traditions meet, naturally. Where flavors don't compete — they complement.

2024

Growing the Experience

We expanded into Dukan SYKO. Because food doesn't stop at the table — it lives in the pantry, in the ingredients, in everyday life. Now, SYKO is a neighborhood space where culture, food, and community come together.

Our Philosophy

We didn't invent fusion.

We lived it.

Every dish we serve comes from real moments, real meals, and real memories.

Syria → Korea → Brooklyn

SYKO began as a family table where Syrian and Korean food met. It grew from early migration histories into a Brooklyn restaurant in 2022, then expanded into Dukan SYKO in 2024, becoming a neighborhood space where culture, food, and community come together.
