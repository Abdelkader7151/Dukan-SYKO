Our Story

How SYKO Started

From early Syrian and Korean migration history to one neighborhood destination in Brooklyn, this is the journey that became SYKO.

During the early 1880s, the first Syrians emigrated to the U.S., leading to the creation of Little Syria on Washington Street.

The first Koreans arrived in 1884, followed by more in 1903, establishing Korean communities in Queens and Manhattan.

How It Became a Place

From History to Neighborhood Home

SYKO brings Syrian and Korean flavors into one identity shaped by Brooklyn street culture, shared hospitality, and the stories of migration.

The Start of SYKO

SYKO launched in Brooklyn, bringing Syrian and Korean traditions together through food. The market experience then expanded with Dukan SYKO, connecting pantry culture and neighborhood life.
